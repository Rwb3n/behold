# Prioritization

Decide what matters most right now by reasoning from constraints, not wishes. Time, energy, context window, and momentum are finite. Prioritization is saying no to good things so the best things happen.

## When to use
- Planning what to work on next from a backlog
- Choosing between competing demands in a session
- Deciding what to cut when everything feels important

## Guiding questions
1. What is the binding constraint right now? (time, energy, information, dependencies)
2. Which item, if done first, unblocks or de-risks the most other items?
3. What is the cost of delay for each option? Which has the steepest penalty?
4. What can be deferred without compounding cost?
5. Does the current ordering reflect actual priorities, or just arrival order?

## Output structure
- Binding constraint identified
- Ranked items with rationale
- What was explicitly deprioritized and why
- First action to take
